Biography

Early Life

Rohit Sharma's early life was marked by financial challenges, leading him to spend time with his grandparents and uncles in Borivali, Mumbai. He was born in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, India, on April 30, 1987. His father was a caretaker of a transport firm storehouse, and his mother is from Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh.

Achievements

🏆 Major International Titles: Won two T20 World Cups (2007 & 2024) – the only Indian to do so. 🏅 National Honors: Awarded the Khel Ratna (India's highest sporting honor) in 2020. 🏏 Batting Records & Stats: Holds the record for the highest individual score in ODIs – 264 runs. 🏅 Leadership & IPL Achievements: Captained Mumbai Indians to 5 IPL titles (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020). 🏅 International Recognition: Named the ICC ODI Player of the Year in 2019. 🏅 Recent Milestones: Led India to victory in the 2024 T20 World Cup. 🏅 Personal Honors: A stand at Wankhede Stadium was named after him in May 2025.

Legacy

Known as the “Hitman” for his effortless six-hitting and stroke play 🏏. One of the most successful IPL captains, leading Mumbai Indians to five titles 🧠. Excelled in all three formats of the game – rare in modern cricket 🏆. Received the Khel Ratna and Arjuna Award for contributions to Indian sports 🇮🇳. Seen as one of the most elegant and dangerous batters globally 🌍. Played a key role in India’s transition after the Dhoni-Kohli era 🧱.
